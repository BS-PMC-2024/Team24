Errors in Software Requirements Specification (SRS) Documents

  1. Ambiguity
    Using vague language that can be interpreted in multiple ways. Avoid terms like "approximately", "normally", "usually", etc.
  2. Incomplete Requirements
    Leaving out essential requirements or failing to address all aspects of the system.
  3. Over-Specification
    Including design or implementation details that constrain developers unnecessarily.
  4. Inconsistency
    Contradictory statements or terminology used inconsistently throughout the document.
  5. Lack of Traceability
    Not linking requirements to their source or related documents, making it difficult to track changes or understand the origin.
  6. Poorly Defined Scope
    Failing to clearly define the boundaries of the system, leading to scope creep.
  7. Missing Non-Functional Requirements
    Omitting requirements related to performance, security, usability, etc.
  8. Lack of Acceptance Criteria
    Not defining how the requirements will be tested and accepted, leading to misunderstandings about what constitutes completion.
  9. Unprioritized Requirements
    Failing to prioritize requirements, which can lead to critical features being overlooked or delayed.
  10. Ignoring Stakeholder Input
    Not involving all relevant stakeholders in the requirements gathering process, leading to overlooked needs and dissatisfaction.
